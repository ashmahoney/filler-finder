Helpful hints:

  • If the filler is in a park or other large public space, give your best guess as to the street address and use the description section to describe what it's near (bathrooms, baseball diamonds, parking lots, etc.)
  • Company refers to the company operating the water filler -- for example, many gallon fillers are operated by Glacier Water. If you can't find the company, no worries!
  • For type: drinking fountains refer to drinking fountains and/or drinking fountains with attached spigots, bottle fillers refer to machines like those in airports that generally have either a button or a sensor and are specifically for filling water bottles, and gallon fillers refer to stations that are only used to fill gallon pitchers. If you can't decide between options, take your best guess.
  • Please stick to fillers in public places. Pretty please. We aren't covering your legal fees if you get caught on private property. We don't even pay to run this website.
