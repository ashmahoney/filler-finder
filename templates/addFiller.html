Helpful hints:

  • Name can refer to the name of the park the filler is in (ex. Central Park), the name of the specific location (ex. Westfield Mall), or just the street address if neither of the other options applies.
  • If the filler is in a park or other large public space, use the closest street address or the latitude/longtitude coordinates (can be found using Google Maps) and use the description section to describe what it's near (bathrooms, fields, parking lots, etc.)
  • Company refers to the company operating the water filler -- for example, many gallon fillers are operated by Glacier Water. If you can't find the company, no worries!
  • For type: drinking fountains refer to drinking fountains with or without attached spigots, bottle fillers refer to machines like those in airports that generally have either a button or a sensor and are specifically for filling water bottles, and gallon fillers refer to stations that are only used to fill gallon pitchers. If you can't decide between options, take your best guess.
  • Please stick to fillers in PUBLIC places. This really should be self explanatory but we are not responsible if you get caught trespassing so please use common sense. :)
